Section 1

The Big Idea

Imagine four detectives investigating the same stock. One studies it minute-by-minute, one in 30-minute chunks, one day-by-day, one week-by-week. Each detective weighs thirteen clues, announces "I'm X% confident this stock is about to rise", and — crucially — keeps score of their own mistakes and recalibrates after every closed case. When several detectives independently agree, each becomes a little bolder (never a lot). A trade happens only when one detective's confidence clears a high, self-adjusting bar and the trade is mathematically worth its costs.

The engine rests on one assumption: markets are approximately self-similar. Auction structure, participation anomalies and flow imbalances carry signal at every timescale — but with different relative importance. So instead of hand-crafting four different strategies, Hermes runs one identical pipeline at four resolutions and lets per-horizon learners discover what matters at each scale.

Design philosophy in three rules

1 · Evidence over prediction

No price targets, no forecasts. Thirteen observable market facts are fused into one probability that a favourable move is underway right now.

2 · Scepticism is the prior

The fusion starts at log-odds −1.0 (≈27% probability). Evidence must overcome scepticism — silence means no trade. Most evaluations end in no trade.

3 · Never hand-tune what can be learned

Priors encode intuition (flow matters intraday, momentum matters positionally), but if reality disagrees, the per-horizon learners migrate the weights.

The Four Horizons

A "horizon" is a zoom level. Each one has its own candle size, its own maximum holding period, its own profit/stop distances (scaled by that zoom level's typical wiggle, the ATR), its own slice of capital, and its own brain. Faster horizons learn faster but see noisier outcomes; slower horizons learn cautiously.

Section 3

The Thirteen Evidence Features

Each clue is a number: positive = bullish, negative = bearish, zero = silent. Before fusing, every clue is squashed with tanh(x/2) so it lives in (−1, +1) — one crazy outlier can never dominate the verdict. The original eight (x₁–x₈) cover auction structure, momentum, regime, and the options plane. V2 adds five more (x₉, x₁₁–x₁₄) for maturity, acceleration, higher-timeframe alignment, and a momentum spectrum — all competing inside the same evidence-mass budget as the original eight.
φᵢ = tanh(xᵢ / 2) every feature, every horizon, same squash

x₁ · Auction Breakout Strength

x₁ = (Close − POC) / ATR

POC = the price where the most volume traded (the market's "fair price" magnet from the volume profile). Trading far above it, measured in ATR units, signals the auction is migrating up.

Example Close 1480, POC 1455, ATR 12 → x₁ = +2.08 → φ₁ = +0.78 (strongly bullish)

x₂ · Value-Area Acceptance

x₂ = (Close − VAH) / ATR

VAH = top of the 70% value area. Closing above it means buyers are accepting higher prices, not just probing them.

Example Close 1480, VAH 1472, ATR 12 → x₂ = +0.67 → φ₂ = +0.32

x₃ · Participation Anomaly

x₃ = z-score of volume over trailing 20 bars

Unusual crowd participation. A move on 3σ volume means far more than the same move on quiet volume.

Example This bar's volume is 2.4σ above its 20-bar mean → φ₃ = +0.83

x₄ · Momentum Quality

x₄ = t-stat of last 20 log returns = mean(r) / (std(r)/√20)

Rewards steady drift; punishes one noisy spike of equal total move. Scale-free, so it means the same thing on 1-min and weekly bars.

Example 20 bars drifting up smoothly: t = +2.1 → φ₄ = +0.78. Same total gain from one spike bar: t ≈ +0.5 → φ₄ = +0.24.

x₅ · Regime Detector (Variance Ratio)

x₅ = ln VR(5), VR(q) = Var(r_q) / (q·Var(r₁))

Lo–MacKinlay test. Trending markets: multi-bar variance grows faster than 1-bar variance → VR > 1 → positive. Mean-reverting chop → negative. Random walk → 0. It tells the engine whether to trust breakout evidence at all.

Example VR = 1.35 → x₅ = +0.30 → φ₅ = +0.15 (mild trending regime)

x₆ · Volumetric Imbalance (options flow)

x₆ = ln(bid_qty / ask_qty) on the ATM call

Are buyers or sellers stacked deeper in the ATM call? Depth imbalance leans toward informed demand for upside.

Example bid 14,300 vs ask 6,100 → x₆ = +0.85 → φ₆ = +0.40

x₇ · Strike-Specific PCR Tilt (Δ-mode)

level = −ln(PE_OI / CE_OI) at ATM strike x₇ = Δlevel over 30 min, self-normalised by EWMA of its own |Δ| (λ=0.97)

Fresh positioning, not stale stock. OI accumulates, so the level is a saturating integral of past flow — near-constant at entry time. The signal lives in the windowed change, made scale-free by self-normalisation. (Battery finding 2026-06-12: level mode had zero discrimination.)

Example −ln(PCR) rose 0.040 in 30 min; typical 30-min change is 0.018 → x₇ = +2.2 → φ₇ = +0.80 (call writers/long-call flow building fast)

x₈ · Variance Risk Premium

x₈ = (RV_h − IV) / IV RV_h = EWMA realised vol (λ=0.94) on the horizon's own bars, annualised with that bar's frequency

Realised motion vs the fear priced into options. RV > IV: the stock moves more than options charge for — cheap convexity environment, favourable. Always compares like with like (each horizon annualises with its own factor: 375×252, 12.5×252, 252, 52).

Example RV 31%, IV 26% → x₈ = +0.19 → φ₈ = +0.10

V2 feature pack (enabled by default — OMNI_V2=1)

x₉ · Maturity / Range Position

chop: x₉ = 2·(1 − 2·(C−LL)/(HH−LL)) over 200 bars trend: x₉ = 1.5 − 0.5·(HH₄₀ − C)/ATR (pullback from swing high) x₉ = g·trend + (1−g)·chop

Regime-conditional: in chop, near the range base is bullish fuel; extended highs are exhaustion. In trend, shallow pullbacks from recent highs are good (52-week-high effect); deep breakdowns are not. Blended by the variance-ratio regime gate g.

x₁₁ · Momentum Acceleration

x₁₁ = t-stat(now) − t-stat(5 bars ago)

The change in momentum quality — an inflection detector. The t-stat level is late by design; its delta fires at the turn (same level→delta lesson as ΔPCR).

x₁₂ · HTF Trend Alignment

intraday ← short_term's mom t-stat (30-min bars) short_term ← swing's mom t-stat (daily bars) swing ← positional's mom t-stat (weekly bars) positional ← own 40-bar secular drift t-stat

Each horizon hears the next-slower horizon's trend as a first-class learnable voice (cached at the source's cadence — negligible API cost). Promotes "trend is your friend" from a whisper to evidence with weight up to 2.5.

Example swing align = +1.4 → φ₁₂ = +0.76 (daily trend supports the long)

x₁₄ · Long Momentum

x₁₄ = t-stat over 5×MOM_WINDOW bars (100 bars)

A second, slower momentum t-stat. Fast (x₄) and slow (x₁₄) momentum become separate learnable voices — making "shallow pullback inside a long advance" visible to the fusion for the first time.

x₁₆ · Volume-Confirmed Momentum

x₁₆ = x₄ · clip(x₃, 0, 2) / 2

Drift on expanding participation persists; on fading volume it goes silent (0), never penalised. An interaction a linear pooler cannot form.

Mass renormalisation. Thirteen features share the original eight-feature weight budget (mass_renorm): richer evidence re-ranks influence but never inflates overall conviction — L's scale stays comparable to v1 and Platt calibration is not crushed.
Degraded mode. For cash-only symbols (or when the chain fetch fails), x₆–x₈ are set to 0 and the engine runs structure-only — but the entry bar is raised to max(p*, 0.65). Thinner evidence must never lower entry strictness.
Section 4

Fusion: from Thirteen Clues to One Calibrated Probability

Think of a sceptical judge. She starts every case at "probably not" (27%). Each clue pushes her up or down, with a learned weight per clue. Then she peeks at the other three detectives: if they agree, she nudges her verdict slightly toward theirs — but the nudge is capped, so peer pressure can never overturn her own evidence. Finally, she corrects for her own historical over/under-confidence before announcing the number.

Step 1 — Regime-gated weighted log-odds (V2 dual books)

g = σ(K · smoothed x₅) K = 2.0, EWMA λ = 0.85 on x₅ w_eff,i = g · w_trend,i + (1−g) · w_chop,i L_h = b₀ + Σᵢ w_eff,i · φ_h,i b₀ = −1.0 (prior ≈ 27%)

V2 maintains two weight books per horizon — trend and chop — blended by the smoothed variance-ratio regime gate. In trending tape the trend book dominates; in chop the chop book does. With OMNI_V2=0 both books move in lockstep (g pinned at 0.5) and fusion reverts to the v1 single-book form.

Step 2 — Bounded cross-horizon coupling

L′_h = L_h + κ · tanh( mean_{g≠h} L_g / 2 ) κ = 0.30

When independent resolutions of the same market agree, every horizon's posterior is nudged that way — but the bonus is bounded by ±κ. Coupling breaks ties; it can never overturn strong local evidence.

Step 3 — Online Platt calibration (with warmup)

p_cal = σ(a·L′ + b) a=1, b=0 at cold start (identity) V2 warmup: identity until 15 closed outcomes (CALIB_MIN_N) after each closed trade or shadow (y = 1 if R>0): a ← a − 0.05·(p̂−y)·L′ bounded a ∈ [0.5, 3] (floor prevents crush) b ← b − 0.05·(p̂−y) bounded b ∈ [−2, 2]

A model that says "65%" but wins 30% of the time is lying to the Kelly sizer and the cost gate. Calibration learns the lie and deflates it: overconfidence shrinks a and pushes b negative. p_cal drives every downstream decision; raw p is logged alongside.

Worked example (swing horizon, core 8 features shown).
φ = [structure +0.78, value +0.32, vol +0.83, mom +0.78, vr +0.15, flow +0.40, pcr +0.80, vrp +0.10, align +0.76, …]
w_eff ≈ swing priors blended by g=0.62 (mild trend regime)
Σ w·φ on core eight = +3.37 · with align +0.76 at w≈0.8 adds another +0.61
L = −1.0 + 3.98 = +2.98 → other horizons' mean L = +0.9 → L′ = 2.98 + 0.30·tanh(0.45) = +3.11
Calibration (a=0.92, b=−0.10): p_cal = σ(0.92·3.11 − 0.10) = 0.94. Strong confluence — and it still has seven gates to survive.
Section 5

Seven Hard Gates — Conviction Is Not Enough

Even at 90% confidence the engine refuses to trade if the trade is structurally bad: fighting a falling higher-timeframe trend, too expensive to execute, too small a target to bother, options too panicked, or it just exited the same setup. Every rejection is counted (gate telemetry), so "why didn't it trade?" is always answerable with data, never a guess. Vetoed entries become shadow trades when every other hard gate would have passed — so blocked cohorts stay auditable forever.

Section 6

Capital Sleeves & Fractional Kelly

The capital (₹10,00,000 paper) is split into four sealed buckets — one per horizon — so a bad week for the fast brain can never drain the slow brain's funds. Within a bucket, bet size follows Kelly's formula for "optimal growth", then takes only a quarter of it (full Kelly is famously violent), and never risks more than 5% of the bucket on one trade.
Sizing worked example (swing). p = 0.72, β = 2.0 → f* = 0.72 − 0.28/2 = 0.58 → quarter-Kelly 0.145 → capped at 0.05 (5% sleeve risk).
Sleeve ₹2,00,000 → risk budget ₹10,000 → stop distance ₹18 → qty = 555 shares (≈₹8.2L notional, risking ₹10k if the initial stop is hit — 1R = ₹10,000).
Section 7

Triple-Barrier Exits with a Conditional Time Stop

Every position is born inside a box with three walls: a profit target above, a stop-loss below, and a time limit ahead. A fourth mechanism — the chandelier trail — drags the stop upward once the trade has moved in our favour, locking in gains. The clever part: the time wall isn't a dumb clock. When time runs out, the engine re-asks the market whether the original thesis is still alive, and only then decides to extend or exit.

V2 late trail arming

With trail_late on (default), the chandelier trail does not arm at a fixed ATR offset alone — it waits until price reaches max(arm_atr, 0.55 × rt) × ATR from entry (≥ 55% of the profit-target distance). This prevents scrap-harvesting: the trail cannot lock in a tiny gain before the trade has earned meaningful progress toward its PT.

The re-underwriting time stop (conditional vertical barrier)

When max-hold is reached, "time elapsed" is replaced by "information decayed":

Live edge

Re-score the thesis with fresh horizon-native evidence. p_now ≥ 0.55 → grant one more hold window; below → exit. The market, not the clock, decides.

Gap replay fallback

No fresh data (outage/restart replay)? Failure-to-perform test: extend only if the trade ever progressed ≥ 0.5R. A moving trade keeps its slot; a sideways zombie is cut.

Extension budgets

intraday 3 · short_term 2 · swing 2 · positional 1. Exhausted → TIME_EXIT regardless of conviction. Budgets persist across restarts.

Bar-walk reconciliation (the crash-safety engine)

Every exit decision replays 1-minute bars newer than the position's last processed bar. Healthy cycle: 1–2 bars. After an outage or restart: the entire gap — trail arming, stop lifts, barrier hits and square-offs all land on the price path that actually happened, never on the post-gap price alone. Replay is idempotent (no bar processed twice) and fills are gap-realistic with pessimistic intra-bar ordering: square-off/time at the open first, then stop before target; a gap through a barrier fills at the open, never at the price the market skipped.

Section 8

The Self-Learning Loop — V2 Adaptations per Horizon

After every closed trade — and every resolved shadow trade (counterfactual evaluations that failed only the p* or align-veto gates) — the owning horizon updates: (1) dual weight books (trend + chop, regime-gated at fusion time), (2) the entry bar p* (losses raise it fast, wins lower it slowly; idle decay releases it toward base when flat), and (3) the Platt calibration map. All thirteen features compete inside the original eight-feature mass budget. The intraday brain never contaminates the positional brain.

Shadow learning (counterfactual trades)

When an evaluation fails only the p* gate (or the align veto) but would have passed every other hard gate, V2 registers an in-memory shadow position walked by the same pessimistic triple-barrier engine. Shadows teach at quarter authority (η × 0.25), further scaled by 1/√N_symbols so a 100-symbol universe cannot stampede shared p*/calibration state. Winning shadows lower p* slightly (recovery channel while flat); losing shadows gently confirm the bar. No orders, no DB rows — pure counterfactual audit.

Weight-update worked example (short_term, η = 0.08, g = 0.7 trend).
Trade closes at R = +1.8. At entry, φ_mom = +0.78, φ_align = +0.60.
mom (trend book, share 0.7): w ← 0.9 × exp(0.08×1.8×0.78×0.7) = 0.978
align (trend book): w ← 0.8 × exp(0.08×1.8×0.60×0.7) = 0.857 (HTF trend spoke and was right)
Then all thirteen weights per book are scaled so their sum returns to the horizon's initial mass — for a feature to grow, another must shrink. Conviction is zero-sum.
Why exponentiated-gradient (and not plain gradient descent)?

EG updates are multiplicative, so weights stay positive and bounded after clipping, and the renormalisation step conserves total evidence mass. The learner can re-rank features but can never inflate overall conviction — a structural guard against the classic failure mode of online learners that grow ever more confident. The asymmetric R-clip [−2, +3] lets big wins teach slightly more than big losses can panic.

Diagnostics: alongside deployed weights, each learner accumulates earned credit per feature (Ση·R·φᵢ before clipping/renormalisation) — the learner's verdict free of dilution. Judge attribution on credit; judge deployment on net Δw. Gate telemetry (GATE_STATS) counts every evaluation outcome, so "why didn't it trade" is measured, not guessed.

Section 9

What's Actually Novel Here

Self-similar multi-horizon design

Not four strategies — one pipeline at four resolutions, with priors encoding scale intuition and learning correcting it. The hypothesis "the same evidence matters differently at different scales" is testable per horizon, in the weights.

Bounded agreement coupling

Ensemble members usually vote or average — Hermes' horizons stay sovereign. κ·tanh(·) lets confluence break ties while making it mathematically impossible for peer pressure to overturn local evidence.

Conditional time barrier

Standard triple-barrier uses a clock. Hermes re-underwrites: at max-hold it re-scores the thesis with fresh evidence and lets the market decide between extension and exit — with hard extension budgets so labels stay bounded.

Evidence-mass conservation

The EG learner renormalises weight mass after every update. Features compete zero-sum for influence; runaway self-confidence is structurally impossible, not just discouraged.

Cost-aware, vol-self-regulating entries

The cost gate expresses friction in R-units of the actual stop distance, so quiet regimes automatically suppress trading and vol expansion re-enables it — no volatility filter needed; it falls out of the arithmetic.

Honesty machinery

Online Platt calibration feeds deflated probabilities to Kelly and the cost gate — with a 15-outcome warmup and an a floor so early noisy samples cannot lock a horizon out. ΔPCR self-normalisation fixes a real battery-discovered flaw (OI levels saturate). Earned-credit diagnostics separate "what the learner believes" from "what renormalisation deployed".

HTF alignment veto + shadow audit

Battery-earned hard gate: no long against φ_align < −0.20. Vetoed entries become shadows, so the rule stays falsifiable — if the market proves the veto wrong, the report says so in numbers.

Regime-gated dual weight books

Trend and chop are different games. V2 learns separate weight vectors for each, blended by a smoothed variance-ratio gate — breakout evidence trusted in trends, mean-reversion respected in chop.

Shadow counterfactual learning

Below-p* evaluations that passed every other gate are walked as phantom trades — teaching at reduced authority without placing orders, and providing a recovery channel for p* while the horizon is flat.

Section 11

Beginner's Glossary

ATR (Average True Range)

The stock's typical bar-to-bar wiggle. Hermes measures every distance (targets, stops, trails) in ATRs, so the same logic adapts to calm and wild stocks automatically.

Log-odds

Probability re-expressed so that adding evidence = adding numbers. 0 ↔ 50%, +2.2 ↔ 90%, −1 ↔ 27%. The sigmoid σ converts back.

Open Interest (OI) & PCR

OI = how many option contracts exist at a strike. PCR = put OI ÷ call OI. Its change reveals where fresh money is positioning right now.

Implied Volatility (IV)

The future turbulence the option price itself implies. High IV = expensive insurance = fear. Hermes refuses entries when ATM IV exceeds the symbol's cap.

Kelly fraction

The mathematically optimal bet size for long-run growth given your edge and payoff. Betting full Kelly is a rollercoaster; Hermes uses a quarter of it with a hard 5% cap.

R-multiple

Profit measured in units of what you risked. Risked ₹10,000, made ₹18,000 → R = +1.8. All learning and reporting is in R, so trades of different sizes are comparable.

Triple barrier

Profit wall, loss wall, time wall. First touch labels the trade. The standard framing for honest trade outcomes (López de Prado), here upgraded with a conditional time wall.

Platt calibration

A tiny two-parameter map that converts a model's raw confidence into honest probability, learned from its own track record. The difference between "I feel 70%" and "history says my 70% wins 70%".

Shadow trade

A counterfactual position registered when conviction (or alignment) blocks entry but every other gate would pass. Walked by the same barrier engine, teaches at reduced authority, never places an order.

HTF alignment

Each horizon's read on the next-slower timescale's trend (momentum t-stat on native bars). Positive = higher timeframe supports the long; below −0.20 triggers the align veto.
